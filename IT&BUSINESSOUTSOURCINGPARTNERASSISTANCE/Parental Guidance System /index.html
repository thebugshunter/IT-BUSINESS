Guidance, Not Surveillance.

Introducing the Parental Guidance System (PGS): a new approach to digital parenting. We empower parents with insights and educational tools to foster dialogue and trust, moving beyond control to build a healthier digital future for families.

Our Core Philosophy

The PGS is built on a foundation of principles designed to support families, not just monitor them. Our goal is to equip parents with the tools for constructive conversation and to create a safer, more transparent digital world for children.

Privacy by Design

The system collects only essential metadata (like who is being contacted), never the content of messages or calls. Privacy is not an afterthought; it's the architecture.

Education Over Enforcement

The primary function is to provide parents with context and communication strategies, transforming data points into opportunities for dialogue.

Empowerment for All

We empower parents with knowledge and children with a safer digital space to grow, learn, and develop responsible online habits.

Age-Appropriate Transparency

Children should understand the system's presence and purpose in a way that is suitable for their age, fostering honesty within the family.

Building Trust

Every feature, from the key transfer at maturity to the non-invasive monitoring, is designed to build, not erode, trust between parents and children.

Secure by Default

Using end-to-end encryption and decentralized keys, we ensure that family data remains private and secure.

The Lifecycle of Trust

The PGS is designed to evolve with your family. It's a temporary support system, not a permanent fixture, with a clear path toward digital independence for your child. Click each stage to learn more.

Stage 1: Activation

Parents undergo identity verification and install the PGS via a secure configuration file on the child's device. This begins the journey with a foundation of security and clear consent.

Stage 2: Ongoing Guidance

Parents use the Hub to view metadata, understand digital habits, and access educational resources to guide conversations. The focus is on dialogue and support.

Stage 3: Growing Up & Deactivation

At an agreed-upon age, the parent securely transfers the encryption key to the child, deactivating all monitoring. This final act completes the circle of trust and grants full digital autonomy.

Navigating the Ethical Landscape

We acknowledge the complexities of digital parenting. This system is designed to be a tool for good, but its success relies on thoughtful implementation by families. We are committed to fostering a healthy balance between safety and autonomy.

Trust vs. Control

The core challenge. By providing educational resources and avoiding content surveillance, we strongly encourage parents to use PGS as a conversation starter, not a control mechanism. Open dialogue about the system's presence is key.

Child Autonomy

A child's right to privacy evolves as they mature. The system's design respects this, from its non-invasive monitoring to the ultimate "key transfer" that ensures a definitive path to digital independence.
